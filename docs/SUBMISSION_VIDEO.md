# BrainGarden Submission Video

## Goal

Win on the strength of one clear promise:

**BrainGarden turns a messy, slow capture workflow into a near-instant save plus automatic knowledge curation.**

Do not make the video about architecture first. Make it about the user payoff:

1. Save something in seconds from any Android app.
2. Store the raw source automatically.
3. Curate it into a usable Obsidian knowledge base without manual filing.

## Recommended Format

- Target length: **70 to 90 seconds**
- Style: **fast product demo with light narration**
- Structure: **problem -> magic moment -> proof -> why it matters**

## Core Message

Most people do not fail at collecting knowledge because they lack tools. They fail because saving is too slow, and organizing later never happens.

BrainGarden fixes both:

- **Quick save** through the native Android Share sheet
- **Automatic storage + curation** into a git-backed Obsidian vault

## Best Story To Tell

Use one concrete example, not three.

Recommended example:

- Find a useful paragraph or article snippet on your phone
- Share it to **BrainGarden**
- Show `Sending capture...`
- Show `Saved to server`
- Cut immediately to the vault:
  - a new file in `raw/`
  - an updated note in `notes/`
  - updated `log.md`
  - updated `overview.md` or `index.md`

That sequence proves the whole idea in under a minute.

## 80-Second Storyboard

### 0 to 8 seconds: Hook

Visual:

- Fast cuts of messy notes, tabs, screenshots, saved links, or a cluttered knowledge workflow
- Then cut to BrainGarden logo/app name

Voiceover:

> We all save things we want to remember, but organizing them later usually never happens.

On-screen text:

- `Saving knowledge is easy. Curating it is the hard part.`

### 8 to 22 seconds: Show the pain

Visual:

- On phone: highlight useful text in an app
- Open Android Share sheet
- Brief pause to imply the normal friction

Voiceover:

> The problem is not capture alone. It is turning a quick save into a knowledge base that stays useful.

On-screen text:

- `Capture is easy`
- `Organization is the bottleneck`

### 22 to 40 seconds: The magic moment

Visual:

- Tap **BrainGarden** in the Share sheet
- Show the BrainGarden share screen with text preview
- Show `Sending capture...`
- Show `Saved to server`

Voiceover:

> With BrainGarden, I share from any Android app, and in seconds the capture is sent to my backend and stored automatically.

On-screen text:

- `Native Android Share flow`
- `Quick save in seconds`

### 40 to 63 seconds: Proof of curation

Visual:

- Screen recording on desktop
- Show the vault repo or Obsidian side by side
- Open:
  - `raw/...txt`
  - one updated note in `notes/`
  - `log.md`
  - `overview.md` or `index.md`

Voiceover:

> But the real value is what happens next. BrainGarden keeps the raw source as ground truth, then automatically curates the content into my Obsidian vault.

On-screen text:

- `Raw source preserved`
- `Curated notes updated automatically`
- `Obsidian vault stays organized`

### 63 to 75 seconds: Why this matters

Visual:

- Show the curated note with a strong title and short summary
- Show related notes or vault navigation

Voiceover:

> So instead of dumping information into a pile, BrainGarden turns each capture into something I can actually find, trust, and reuse later.

On-screen text:

- `From capture -> knowledge`

### 75 to 85 seconds: Close

Visual:

- Return to logo or clean product shot
- Optional split screen: phone capture on left, curated vault on right

Voiceover:

> BrainGarden is a faster way to save, and a smarter way to grow a personal knowledge base.

On-screen text:

- `BrainGarden`
- `Quick save. Automatic curation.`

## Short Voiceover Script

Use this if you want a single clean read:

> We all save things we want to remember, but organizing them later usually never happens. BrainGarden solves both sides of that problem. I can share text from any Android app using the native Share sheet, and BrainGarden saves it to my backend in seconds. Then it automatically stores the raw source, updates my curated notes, and keeps my Obsidian vault organized. Instead of a pile of random captures, I get a knowledge base that keeps itself useful. BrainGarden is quick to save, automatic in curation, and built to turn capture into lasting knowledge.

## Recording Plan

Record these clips in order:

1. Android phone: select useful text in any app.
2. Android phone: open Share sheet and tap `BrainGarden`.
3. Android phone: capture the `Sending capture...` state.
4. Android phone: capture the `Saved to server` state.
5. Desktop: open the vault and reveal the new raw file in `raw/`.
6. Desktop: open the updated curated note in `notes/`.
7. Desktop: open `log.md`.
8. Desktop: open `overview.md` or `index.md`.
9. End card: BrainGarden logo plus tagline.

## Editing Notes

- Keep cuts tight. Do not leave dead air in the Share sheet.
- Add zoom or highlight when the status changes from `Sending capture...` to `Saved to server`.
- When showing the vault, spend the most time on the curated note. That is the payoff.
- Use subtitles even if you also record voiceover.
- Put the words **Quick save** and **Automatic curation** on screen at least once each.

## What Judges Should Remember

If the judges remember only one sentence, it should be this:

**BrainGarden makes knowledge capture fast enough to use in real life and organizes it automatically so it stays valuable later.**

## What Not To Do

- Do not spend the first half explaining backend architecture.
- Do not show too many features.
- Do not use multiple examples unless the video is longer than 90 seconds.
- Do not describe it as “just a note-taking app.”
- Do not end on implementation details when you can end on user value.
